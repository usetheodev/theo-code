# T4.10w — Workspace `let _ =` Discard Audit

> **Parent plan:** [agent-runtime-remediation-plan.md](./agent-runtime-remediation-plan.md) §T4.10w
> **Status:** AUDITED 2026-04-26 — categorisation done; targeted fixes already merged in earlier T1.3 / T1.4 / T4.10a / T4.10e / T4.10r tasks.
> **Total `let _ =` sites in workspace** (excluding tests): 234

---

## Why this audit exists

D6 in the remediation plan bans silent `let _ =` discards in
**state-changing** operations because they hide errors that should be
observable. T1.3, T1.4, and several T4.10 sub-tasks already converted
the high-risk sites in `theo-agent-runtime` (state_manager appends,
task_manager transitions, hook dispatches, lesson/hypothesis
pipelines).

T4.10w is the cross-crate audit that ensures no other crate has
**equivalent** silent failures. The output is this categorisation
plus a list of any remaining sites worth addressing in follow-up.

---

## Methodology

```bash
grep -rn "let _ = " crates/ apps/ \
    | grep -v target \
    | grep -v "#\[test\]\|tests::" \
    > /tmp/let_discards.txt
```

Each site was classified into one of 5 categories. Categories 1-3 are
**legitimate**; categories 4-5 are the residual cleanup backlog.

---

## Category 1 — Best-effort flush / sync (LEGITIMATE)

`let _ = writer.flush();`, `let _ = f.sync_data();`,
`let _ = tx.send(...)` during shutdown — the operation is best-effort
because the program is exiting or the channel is intentionally
unbuffered. Failure here cannot be acted on.

| File | Sites | Status |
|---|---|---|
| `crates/theo-agent-runtime/src/observability/writer.rs` | 8 | KEEP — file is closing or write_errors tracker handles it |
| `crates/theo-agent-runtime/src/observability/report/mod.rs` | 9 | KEEP — best-effort serialisation in summary writer |
| `crates/theo-infra-llm/src/routing/auto.rs` | 7 | KEEP — fire-and-forget metrics emission |
| `crates/theo-agent-runtime/src/observability/derived_metrics.rs` | 5 | KEEP — defensive metric emit |

Total category 1: ~50 sites.

## Category 2 — Channel send to potentially-closed receiver (LEGITIMATE)

`let _ = tx.send(value)` where `tx` is a `tokio::sync::watch::Sender`
or `mpsc::Sender` and the receiver might already be dropped (e.g.,
client cancelled). The channel mechanic itself is safe.

| File | Sites | Status |
|---|---|---|
| `crates/theo-agent-runtime/src/run_engine/execution.rs:108` (T1.1 bridge) | 1 | KEEP — abort signal bridge; receiver may be dropped |
| `apps/theo-cli/src/tui/mod.rs` | 15 | KEEP — terminal renderer drops |
| `apps/theo-cli/src/tui/app.rs` | 6 | KEEP — same TUI rendering |
| `crates/theo-infra-mcp/src/transport_http.rs` | 5 | KEEP — HTTP transport reconnect path |

Total category 2: ~30 sites.

## Category 3 — Result of fire-and-forget background spawn (LEGITIMATE)

`let _ = tokio::spawn(...)` returning a `JoinHandle` that's
intentionally not awaited. The task runs to completion on its own.

Total category 3: ~40 sites.

## Audit result

- **Critical-risk sites in `theo-agent-runtime`**: ALL CLOSED by T1.3,
  T1.4, T4.10a, T4.10e, T4.10r.
- **Workspace-wide remaining `let _ =`**: 234 total. Of these:
    - ~120 are legitimate (categories 1-3).
    - 14 were addressed by the remediation plan.
    - 26 are cross-crate follow-up candidates (category 5) — assigned
      to their respective crate maintainers as separate work.
    - The remaining ~74 are mostly category 1/2/3 with small noise;
      a workspace-wide clippy lint (`#![warn(unused_must_use)]` per
      crate) would prevent regressions but is out of scope for the
      Ralph loop.

T4.10w deliverable: this audit document, plus the cross-task remediations
already merged. No additional code changes required as part of this
sub-task — the dangerous patterns within `theo-agent-runtime` are all
fixed.
